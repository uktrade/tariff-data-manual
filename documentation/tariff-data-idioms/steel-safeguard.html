Steel safeguard

  • Categories
  • Geography
  • Quarterly behaviour
    • Post-exhaustion
    • Volume carry-over

  • Data anatomy
    • Quarter 4 quotas set-up

Steel safeguards aim to prevent dumping of steel-based products by placing a cap on the volume of steel that can be imported. The cap is fine-grained and is controlled on a quarterly basis.

A safeguard set-up is made up of a punitive duty that applies to all imports and then a quota that can override that duty for a set quarterly import volume. Once the quota is exhausted, the punitive duty applies and the import of steel products is strongly abated.

The override behaviour is not possible using the regular tariff rules and preference codes have to be used to allow the override to happen.

One quota then applies for each category for each quarter such that all the products in that category draw down from the same volume.

For each product in a category therefore receives one measure for the safeguard and then another measure for that category’s quota.

Where the country has an “X” then it is subject to safeguards in that category. Where the country has a blank then it is exempt from safeguards in that category.

Some of the big exporters in each category get quotas of their own. As well as there being a quota for “all countries” there can also be separate quotas for individual geographies. These big exporters are therefore exempt from the all countries quota.

Quarterly behaviour

Safeguard quotas are defined on a quarterly basis, with “quarter 1” being from July–August and the next quarters continuing from there.

The same quota order numbers are used across each quarter but the volume is refreshed, apart from in quarter 4 which has special behaviour:

  • Quotas for specific countries retain their quota order number in Q4 (so these quota order numbers are used all year).
  • Quotas for “all countries” get a new quota order number in Q4 because of the exhaustion mechanism below.

Post-exhaustion

When the quota for a specific country is exhausted in Q4, a “post-exhaustion” quota opens which uses a completely separate quota order number. The country may shares this post-exhaustion quota with the other countries listed specifically apart from in categories 1 and 4B where it will have a post-exhaustion quota of its own.

The “post-exhaustion” quota is linked to the “all countries” quota for Q4 and can draw down from the all countries volume. The maximum volume they can draw may be capped at some percentage of the all countries volume (but this cap does not mean they are entitled to that volume).

For example, if the all countries volume has an initial volume of 1,000 tonnes, the post-exhaustion quota can be given a volume of 700 tonnes so that it only has 70% of the volume available. If all the other countries have used 400 tonnes then there will only be 600 tonnes left in the shared quota despite the volume of the country-specific post-exhaustion quota being 700 tonnes. The country would therefore only be able to use a maximum of 600 tonnes.

The “post-exhaustion” mechanism isn’t used for categories 5, 16, 20 and 27 - once the country-specific quotas are used there is no further volume available.

Volume carry-over

Any remaining volume left in quota pots in Q3 is automatically added on to quota volumes in Q4 (this is handled by downstream border systems and so doesn’t require changes to tariff data).

Data anatomy

This diagram shows a slightly simplified example of a commodity that is subject to steel safeguards. The key features of this example hypothetical category are:

  • India is exempt from the category – and is therefore excluded from both the safeguard measure and quota.
  • Turkey is a big exporter – it receives its own quota and is exempt from the “all countries” quota.
  • There is only one commodity – bigger categories will have more than one and all will link to the same quotas.

The quota definitions are not shown but would as discussed span a single quarter of the year. The measures are valid for as long as the same quota order number is in use – for categories where a different quota number is used for Q4, they would be valid from Q1 to Q3. The safeguard measure does not have an end date.
