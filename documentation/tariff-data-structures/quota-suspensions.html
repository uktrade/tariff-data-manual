Quota suspensions

  • What quota suspensions are
  • When is a quota not a quota?

  • Business rules

Quota suspensions invalidate a quota; during the suspension period the suspended First Come First Serve (FCFS) quotas are unavailable to traders.

A quota provides an opportunity for an importer to import goods at a rate that is lower than the prevailing MFN (or preferential rate) until the specified volume of goods has run out (or exhausted).

Once the volume has exhausted, the quota is no longer available and the out-of-quota-rate is then payable.

A quota can be managed via two regimes, as follows: First Come First Served (FCFS) and Licensed.

Quota suspensions are applicable only to First Come First Serve quotas.

Learn more about quotas.

What quota suspensions are

  • Quota suspensions are functions that are enabled in legal text to allow for First Come First Serve quotas to be made effectively unavailable to traders for a given period of time, for a number of reasons – these reasons are typically written into the initial legal text, such that there is no requirement to legislate to bring in the suspension.
  • Some typical reasons are as follows:
    • The administrative costs of running the quota mean that there is no value in doing so while the MFN rate is equal to or lower than the in-quota rate – for example, the image to the below is a fragment of EU regulation “2016/1219, amending Council Regulation (EC) No 499/96 as regards Union tariff quotas for certain fish and fishery products originating in Iceland” – the footnote explains why the quota is suspended from 15 Feb to 15 June.
    • e.g. for fishing quotas, a quota is applicable only while overall catch volumes do not exceed a given amount.

Logical model of a quota suspension

  • Using the herrings example from the previous page, quota 09.0792 runs annually from January 1st to December 31st and is applied to commodity code 0303510000.
  • The MFN is 15.00% all year round, except for the period between Feb 15th and June 15th, where it is 0.00% (tariff free)
  • The EU has chosen to suspend the quota for the period where the MFN is zero, as there is no value in running a quota when there is no material gain to be made for the trader in applying for quota usage during that period.

When is a quota not a quota?

A quota suspension can be thought of as an antidote to a quota; a nullification of its effects; during the period of the suspension, it is as if the quota is not there; a trader cannot request an allocation against that quota and it is invisible on the Trade Tariff Service, as follows:

For 1st July 2019, after the suspension, the quota is visible https://www.trade-tariff.service.gov.uk/commodities/0303510010?currency=EUR&day=1&month=7&year=2019#import

For 1st March, during the suspension, the quota is hiddenhttps://www.trade-tariff.service.gov.uk/commodities/0303510010?currency=EUR&day=1&month=7&year=2019#import

It is important to note however that there is more than one way to implement quota suspensions. In this example, the EU has also made the quota measures last from 16th June to 14th Feb and not all year long.

Simplified quotas data model (as applies to suspension periods)

A quota order number is made up of many quota definition periods

It’s important to understand that a single quota order number record is made up of many definition periods: these periods may be of any length (normally no longer than a year, with the most typical lengths being annual and quarterly, as well as a number of custom periods, especially re: agricultural goods & seasonal marketing periods).

Quota suspension data object in detail

The quota suspension object is not one of the more complex objects in the specification: there are few fields, in fact there are only three that you need to actively define.

A suspension period must fit within a definition period

The critical point is that a quota suspension is defined against a quota definition period: the suspension period MUST NOT straddle the boundary of the definition – in the rare occasion you need to straddle a boundary, create 2 suspension
